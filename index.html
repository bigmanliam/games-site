What is Daily-le?

Daily-le is a collection of free geography and trivia games that refresh every day. Each game gives you a new puzzle at midnight your local time — guess a flag, estimate a population, name a capital, or figure out the distance between two cities. Every game uses the same simple format: a handful of guesses, helpful feedback after each attempt, and a shareable emoji scorecard so you can compare with friends.

How It Works

Pick a game from the grid above and start playing — no accounts, no sign-ups, no downloads. Your progress is saved in your browser so you can come back later in the day to finish. After you complete the daily puzzle, most games offer a practice mode for unlimited rounds. When you are done, hit share to copy your emoji board and challenge someone else.

Why Geography Games?

Most people can name fewer than half the world's countries, let alone their capitals or populations. Daily geography games are a low-effort way to build genuine world knowledge over time. After a few weeks of playing, you will notice yourself recognizing flags, estimating populations with surprising accuracy, and placing cities on the map without thinking about it. It is the kind of learning that happens naturally when something is fun.

Tips for Getting Better

Start with what you know and work outward. If you can place the ten most populous countries, you already have anchors for estimating everywhere else. Pay attention to patterns — West African flags often use green, yellow, and red; Scandinavian flags share the Nordic cross; former British colonies frequently incorporate the Union Jack or similar elements. Every game you play adds a few more data points to your mental map.
